Eventvods and me

Eventvods

It all started here.

Back in December 2013 I volunteered to help update content on a subreddit called /r/loleventvods, at the time eventvods was only covering league of legends (hence the "lol"). Things started off slow but at the same time was very hectic, heading straight into off-season I was among a handful of people who took things more or less seriously than I did. Once Spring split 2014 began I was updating and covering multiple leagues and dozens of games a split.

After spring 2014 things began to ramp up, league started gaining popularity at an exponential rate and that meant a lot more people wanted spoiler-free vods. We began experimenting and expanding our base into other established e-sports as well including; dota 2, CS:GO and even heartstone. Our goal was simple. Become the premiere content provider for all spoiler-free vods across all e-sports. There was just one problem, we didn't have the manpower to sustain such a model. So we looked towards moving our base of operations off reddit and into our own website. This would allow us to have full control over our own content.

Our first 3 goals

  1. Topple all other youtube channel competitors
  2. Establish a line of communication with all event organizers
  3. Create original content that would increase viewership and our viewerbase

Our biggest challenges

  • Having consistent, reliable content updaters
  • Keeping up with all the scheduling of e-sport events across all games
  • Dealing with reddit's limited subreddit functions
